# 🤖 The Human-AI Collaboration: Building Mono Supplies

This isn't just another repo; it's a "silent" partnership. Throughout the development of **Mono Supplies**, I worked alongside an AI (Antigravity/Gemini) to see if we could create something that didn't just *work*, but *felt* premium. 

Here’s the story of how it happened.

---

### The Beginning: Blueprinting the Foundation
One of the biggest strengths of our collaboration was the **architectural phase**. I used the AI as a sounding board to design the entire product architecture, from the Next.js App Router structure down to the Supabase database schema. It helped me map out how products, categories, and pricing tiers would relate to each other, ensuring the technical foundation was rock-solid.

### The Design: Human Vision vs. AI "Averages"
While the AI was great at code, its design suggestions initially felt "regular"—functional, but lacklustre. To get that **Quiet Luxury** feel, I had to step in as the lead designer. I spent some time scouring the internet for high-end references and editorial influences to pivot away from a generic B2B look.

Several of the site's "signature" moves were entirely my call:
- **The Requisition Cart**: I decided to build a professional, custom requisition system instead of a standard retail cart, specifically to handle the high-volume inquiry flow needed for commercial hotel procurement.
- **Editorial Video Backgrounds**: I decided to ditch static headers for immersive, HD video backgrounds to set the tone immediately.
- **Prestige Trust Markers**: Instead of generic icons, I chose to use real hotel logos in the "Trusted By" marquee to establish instant institutional authority.
- **The "Shop the Room" Scenes**: I conceptualized the live collection showcase, where products are displayed within a real hotel room setting, giving buyers a cinematic context for the items.

### The Grunt Work: AI as my Lead Engineer
I leaned on the AI for the heavy lifting of building out components and boilerplate:
- **Plumbing**: Generating the foundation for the product modals and the requisition context.
- **Data Fetching**: Typing out the Supabase client logic and ensuring type safety across the board.
- **Framer Motion Logic**: The AI handled the complex math for transitions, while I focused on the "feel" of each animation.

### The Polish: Perfection in the Details
The final 10% was where I did the most manual work. I tweaked every single element to ensure perfect **Mobile Responsiveness**:
- **Navigation**: I specifically chose and styled the hamburger menu to be discrete and functional.
- **UI Calibration**: I manually adjusted the size, orientation, and padding of "Add to Cart" and "View Details" buttons for mobile screens to ensure they felt balanced and easy to use on the go.
- **Layout & Spacing Overrides**: I had to manually override the AI's suggestions to achieve better layout and space utilization in both desktop and mobile views, ensuring a more professional and efficient use of the viewport.
- **Geometric Consistency**: I insisted on the strict **4px corner radius** across every component, overriding the AI's tendency to use standard variables.

### The AI Toolkit
This project was built with a specialized suite of tools, used collectively to bring the vision to life:
- **Claude Code**: Used for the overall product architecture and high-level reasoning.
- **Antigravity with Stitch**: Handled the foundational UI design, component styling, and complex component logic.
- **Cursor**: Used for boilerplate generation and other specific implementations within the IDE.

### The Verdict
The result is a codebase that feels surprisingly human. While the AI acted as a powerful architect and engineer for the foundation, the **soul, design direction, and final polish** were entirely driven by my vision for what a premium B2B platform should be.
